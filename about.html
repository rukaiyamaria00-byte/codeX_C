About codeX

The Journey of a Program

codeX is a sleek, web-based companion for mastering compiler design — fast, intuitive, and built for learners who want results without the headache. Designed with both students and educators in mind, codeX streamlines the learning process by removing manual complexity and delivering instant, accurate outputs for essential compiler construction tasks. With just a few clicks, users can eliminate Left Recursion, apply Left Factoring, generate First() and Follow() sets, and analyze LR(0) parsing with ease. Whether you're preparing for exams, debugging grammar rules, or building your own parser, codeX helps you visualize the entire journey from grammar to parser with clarity and speed. No more error-prone steps. No more guesswork. Just clean, structured results — in seconds.

What You Get

    In this project, users can instantly compile Left Factoring to refactor common‐prefix rules, automatically eliminate Left Recursion to prevent infinite loops in top‐down parsing, compute FIRST and FOLLOW sets to reveal which terminals can appear at the start or immediately after any nonterminal, and execute a full LR(0) parse to drive bottom‐up grammar analysis—all with a single click. The result is a clean, unambiguous grammar, interactive visualizations of your parse steps, and downloadable reports that make it easy to integrate your transformed grammar into any compiler or teaching material.
